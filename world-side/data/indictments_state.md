## 4. Historical retaliation pattern — three indicative cases

Indictments of named state-affiliated cyber operators are not generally treated by their parent service as a deterrent. The historical record shows the opposite — public naming has been followed in multiple instances by escalation in the named unit's operations. Three illustrative cases:

### 4.1 GRU Mueller indictment (13 Jul 2018) → continued GRU operations and 2018 OPCW intrusion attempt
On 13 July 2018, Special Counsel Robert Mueller indicted 12 GRU officers from Units 26165 and 74455 for the 2016 DNC/DCCC intrusions and election-system targeting. The indictment named individual officers including Anatoliy Kovalev. Three months later (4 October 2018), Dutch military intelligence (MIVD) publicly disrupted a GRU Unit 26165 close-access operation against the Organisation for the Prohibition of Chemical Weapons (OPCW) in The Hague — the same officers continued to operate. In October 2020, DOJ filed a follow-on indictment against six GRU Unit 74455 (Sandworm) officers including Kovalev for the NotPetya attack (June 2017, which targeted Ukraine but caused ~$10B global damage), the 2018 Olympics OlympicDestroyer attack, and the BadRabbit campaign — confirming the named unit continued offensive ops at scale through and after the public naming. ([NPR coverage](https://www.npr.org/2020/10/19/925423705/doj-unveils-more-sweeping-cyber-charges-against-russian-intelligence-officers))

### 4.2 APT10 / MSS Tianjin SSB indictment (20 Dec 2018) → continued long-tail operations
On 20 December 2018, SDNY indicted **Zhu Hua** and **Zhang Shilong** (APT10 / Red Apollo) tied to Huaying Haitai and the MSS Tianjin State Security Bureau, for the Cloud Hopper managed-service-provider campaign. The Five Eyes issued contemporaneous joint attribution. APT10 did not stand down — subsequent reporting from 2019 through 2024 documented continued Cloud Hopper-style supply-chain intrusions by APT10 and successor MSS contractors. Lawfare and the American Journal of International Law have characterised the indictment-as-deterrence strategy as having "failed" given the volume and sophistication of subsequent PRC operations attributed to the same MSS bureaus. The current March 2025 i-Soon / APT27 indictment follows the identical structural template — and 2024–2025 saw Salt Typhoon, Volt Typhoon, and the US Treasury breach all attributed to closely related MSS contractor pools. ([Lawfare — Failure of the Chinese-Hacking Indictment Strategy](https://www.lawfaremedia.org/article/failure-united-states-chinese-hacking-indictment-strategy))

### 4.3 Park Jin Hyok / Lazarus indictment (6 Sep 2018) → continued financial-sector operations escalation
On 6 September 2018, DOJ unsealed charges against **Park Jin Hyok** (Lazarus Group) for the 2014 Sony Pictures attack (itself a retaliatory operation against *The Interview*), 2016 Bangladesh Bank SWIFT theft ($81M), and 2017 WannaCry. The indictment-as-attribution did not deter — Lazarus accelerated. February 2021 follow-on DOJ indictment added two more North Korean operators (**Jon Chang Hyok**, **Kim Il**) and detailed continued operations: $1.3B+ attempted theft from banks/cryptocurrency exchanges, continued attacks on entertainment and defense targets, and the Marine Chain ICO fraud. The 21 Feb 2025 **Bybit hack** ($1.5B in crypto stolen — the largest single financial cybercrime in history, FBI-attributed to TraderTraitor / Lazarus / APT38) demonstrates the trajectory: the same parent organization the US named in 2018 continues to escalate, with the most extreme single heist occurring 6½ years after the named indictment. ([FBI PSA on Bybit](https://www.ic3.gov/psa/2025/psa250226))

### Pattern summary for the forecaster

- Indictments do not deter; in the cases above the named unit's operational tempo either held steady or **increased** in the 12–36 months following naming.
- The 30–180-day window after a high-profile naming has been the most consistent retaliation window historically (OPCW operation came 12 weeks after Mueller; Lazarus financial-sector operations consistently spiked in the year following each new public attribution).
- Retaliation is rarely 1:1 attribution-for-attribution. It tends to manifest as: (a) an escalated operation against the indicting state's allies (UK, Netherlands, Ukraine), (b) a high-impact destructive operation against an unrelated soft target, or (c) a financially motivated theft (DPRK pattern).
- Indictments combined with sanctions and rewards (the 2024 LockBit / Khoroshev pattern, the 2025 i-Soon pattern) appear to provoke a stronger reaction than indictments alone.
